90-day entry plan

Install closure before scaling reinvention.

A tactical entry sequence for learning Zeb’s operating frequency, making executive work legible, proving one AI-native CEO-office mechanism, and earning the right to redesign functions.

Day-90 outcome: one functioning CEO-office cadence; one decision-ready intelligence layer; one cross-functional priority shipped or explicitly stopped; one functional redesign in measured pilot; and a board-prep path with clear ownership and definitions.

Operating Principles

Observe before simplifying

Founder intensity often contains high-value pattern recognition. Separate productive detail from avoidable drag.

Write the contract

Every cadence, brief, agent, and initiative needs a purpose, owner, authority boundary, and evidence standard.

Close one loop

Proof beats broad transformation theater. Ship one mechanism and one redesign before scaling claims.

Days 0–15 · Read the Frequency

LISTEN

CEO attention and decision ethnography

  • Shadow product reviews, customer calls, leadership sessions, and priority intake.
  • Capture what Zeb asks, what changes his mind, what he revisits, and what he personally repairs.
  • Distinguish decision-grade detail from recurring noise.
MAP

Current operating topology

  • Map weekly leadership syncs, MBR inputs, quarterly planning, board prep, internal communications, and cross-functional escalations.
  • Identify duplicate reporting, missing ownership, stale decisions, and processes that exist only because they always have.
BASELINE

Measure without pretending certainty

  • Establish initial measures: decision latency, blocker age, priority closure, briefing burden, rework, and CEO time allocation.
  • Label estimates and hypotheses until internal data validates them.

Days 16–30 · Launch the CEO Office Minimum Viable System

Daily CEO Brief v1

Decision queue, strategic risk, customer/product signal, cross-functional exceptions, and what changed.

Executive Commit v1

One record for intent, decision, work split, owner, dependencies, finish line, and evidence.

Weekly leadership rhythm

Agent-synthesized pre-read; meeting time reserved for decisions, contradiction, and unblock work.

Days 31–45 · Prove the Mechanism

Priority closure test

  • Select one cross-functional initiative with real consequence.
  • Define human authority and agent-default work.
  • Publish owner, due state, blocker path, and evidence.
  • Ship, stop, or explicitly reframe.

CEO attention test

  • Compare preparation time and rework.
  • Measure decisions advanced per briefing.
  • Track repeated questions and missing context.
  • Adjust push, pull, watch, and silence rules.

Agent / Human Operating Contract

Days 46–60 · Launch One Functional Redesign

Choose the value stream

Visible friction, willing accountable leader, recurring work, and measurable consequence.

Decompose from first principles

Eliminate, agentize, retain for human judgment, or rebuild as a new path.

Bound the release

Permissions, audit logs, human approval, rollback, exception handling, and adoption ownership.

Measure actual value

Labor hours avoided, cycle time, rework, quality, adoption, and leader capacity reclaimed.

Days 61–75 · Put Board Prep on Rails

  • Establish one narrative owner, source-of-truth definitions, prior-action closure, issue log, review sequence, and dry run.
  • Connect AI-era operating claims to product proof, economics, human authority, risk, and measurable workflow change.
  • Reconcile CEO, CFO, product, and functional narratives before materials become presentation polish.

Days 76–90 · Publish the Operating Contract

  • Codify CEO Brief, Executive Commit, leadership cadence, priority portfolio, and redesign review.
  • Report what was eliminated, what agents now own, what humans still own, what shipped, and what remains unproven.
  • Choose the next two redesigns based on evidence and leader readiness—not symbolic coverage.
