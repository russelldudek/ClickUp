Dear Zeb and the ClickUp hiring team,

ClickUp is not hiring a conventional Chief of Staff. The work is to make an intensely product-oriented CEO more informed and faster while reducing—not adding—the organizational drag between a priority, a decision, and a shipped result. It also requires turning the 100x organization from a provocative thesis into a repeatable operating system across leadership cadence, functional redesign, board preparation, and human-agent work.

That problem is unusually aligned with how I operate. At Vape-Jet, I lead AI-first transformation across production, purchasing, quality, customer success, support, engineering issue flow, ERP/Odoo, Jira, HubSpot, Slack, telemetry, and AI-assisted workflows. I work directly in the mechanisms: information flow, standard work, decision visibility, training, escalation, and accountability. Through DudeWorth, I design agentic workflow patterns that combine structured context, retrieval, agents, human judgment, evaluation, governance, and explicit escalation.

Earlier, at Amazon, I built operating cadence and execution mechanisms in a 24/7 customer-backward environment supporting approximately five million second-day orders annually. At Compunetics, I led across engineering, manufacturing, quality, customer delivery, and high-consequence technical programs. The common thread is not a title. It is the ability to make complex work legible, connect technical and business teams, and convert ambiguous priorities into owned operating outcomes.

My candidate thesis: preserve founder frequency while eliminating organizational drag. I would do that through an “Executive Commit”—a concise operating record that closes intent, decision authority, agent/human boundaries, ownership, evidence, and learning as one transaction.

The independent candidate vision at https://russelldudek.github.io/ClickUp/ makes that reasoning tangible through the Executive Commit interaction, a first-90-day installation sequence, role-specific questions, and a verified evidence map. It is not an imitation of ClickUp’s product or internal systems; it is a demonstration of how I would approach the work.

I would welcome the chance to demonstrate my AI-native workflow directly and discuss the hardest practical question in this role: how to preserve a founder’s exceptional product depth while making the rest of the company faster, clearer, and more accountable.

Carpe diem,
