Executive Commit Review

From “Zeb said” to shipped.

A role-specific review mechanism for converting founder intent into one coherent decision, agent/human work split, accountable delivery path, and evidence return.

Use when: a cross-functional priority, customer/product signal, function redesign, or board-critical issue would otherwise diffuse across teams, documents, meetings, and status updates.

1 · Commit Definition

IntentWhat changes? Why now? What is outside scope?Owner writes one sentence.
DecisionWhat tradeoff is being made? Who has authority? What condition reopens the decision?Decision, not discussion.
Work splitWhat is agent-default? What remains human-required? What triggers escalation?Authority stays explicit.
DeliveryWho owns the outcome? What dependencies and unblock paths matter? What is “shipped”?One accountable owner.
EvidenceWhat outcome, adoption, quality, risk, and learning determine the next decision?No unsupported score.

2 · Executive Review Questions

Value

  • What gets better for a customer, employee, or business outcome?
  • What work disappears—not merely moves?

Authority

  • Who can decide without another meeting?
  • Which decision may an agent never make?

Coherence

  • Which teams experience the consequence?
  • Where could local optimization damage the whole?

Proof

  • What observable result closes this commit?
  • What evidence would make us stop or redesign?

3 · Disposition

StateMeaningRequired evidence
COMMIT TO SHIPDecision and authority are sufficient; delivery path is credible.Owner, finish line, dependencies, and measures named.
REDESIGN FIRSTThe process or work model is the problem.First-principles decomposition and bounded pilot.
HOLD FOR SIGNALConsequence is real but evidence is insufficient.Specific evidence request, owner, and revisit date.
STOPWork does not justify attention or duplicates a better path.Decision recorded; dependencies and stakeholders closed.

Illustrative Commit

Scenario: a CEO-generated product and customer priority touches Product, GTM, Finance, and Operations. The example is illustrative and does not claim knowledge of ClickUp’s internal process or data.

IntentResolve the customer problem and validate the business implication without creating a parallel priority stream.Non-goal: “do everything now.”
DecisionProduct leader classifies the signal as immediate fix, discovery input, strategic bet, operating defect, or watch item.CEO sees the disposition and tradeoff.
Work splitAgents retrieve comparable signals, release history, dependencies, and open commitments. Humans judge product meaning, customer consequence, and priority.Human authority: product tradeoff.
DeliveryOne accountable owner publishes the response date, dependency path, and definition of shipped back to the affected teams.Escalate if authority or evidence conflicts.
EvidenceDecision latency, repeat-signal frequency, customer response, shipped change, adoption, and observed business outcome.Learning returns to product and CEO brief.

Anti-Bureaucracy Guardrails

No duplicate reporting

The commit is the current operating record, not a summary of another system.

No agent theater

AI must remove work, improve decisions, or strengthen evidence—not generate more content.

No endless “in progress”

Every review ends in ship, redesign, hold for specified signal, or stop.

Success Test

After repeated use, the CEO should have better detail with less preparation burden; leaders should know what they own; agents should carry more recurring context work; cross-functional priorities should close faster; and the company should learn from shipped work rather than from status narration.
