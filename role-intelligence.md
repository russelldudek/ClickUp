# Role Intelligence — ClickUp Chief of Staff to the CEO

## Nominal role
Chief of Staff to CEO Zeb Evans.

## Actual work
Build an AI-native CEO office that preserves founder product depth without turning the CEO into the bottleneck; converts fast-generated priorities into shipped cross-functional outcomes; rewires functional work around agents and irreplaceable human judgment; and makes executive, board, and company narrative coherent.

## Company moment
- ClickUp publicly describes itself as the convergence of software, AI, and humans.
- Super Agents are embedded in the workspace as permissioned, auditable users with human approval for high-impact actions.
- Brain² reinforces the company's 100x productivity thesis.
- ClickUp has publicly passed $300M ARR and has discussed a future IPO.
- Recent public reporting on a 22% workforce reduction and 100x-org restructuring increases the internal proof, trust, and change burden. Treat this as external public context, not internal fact.

## Operating tensions
1. Founder detail vs scalable attention.
2. Agent speed vs explicit human authority.
3. Priority volume vs coherent closure.
4. First-principles reinvention vs organizational legitimacy and trust.
5. Product/customer depth vs cross-functional whiplash.
6. Founder velocity vs pre-IPO governance and board rigor.

## Decision architecture
The role should make it easier to decide what changes, what stops, what agents own, what humans own, who has authority, what requires CEO attention, what gets escalated, and what counts as shipped.

## Stakeholders
CEO; leadership team; Product; Engineering; GTM; Finance; People; Legal/Compliance; Operations; customers; board/investors; agent/platform owners.

## Success measures
CEO attention quality; priority closure; cross-functional blocker age; function-redesign cycle time and labor/rework effects; agent adoption and auditability; leadership decision latency; board-prep reliability; customer/product signal closure.

## Change burden
Leaders must expose workflows, give up duplicate reporting, accept explicit decision rights, separate judgment from recurring work, own adoption, and stop treating activity as progress.
